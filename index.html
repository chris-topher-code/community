Hospital & Healthcare in China

GUIDE · 12 MIN READ · UPDATED 2026

China's public hospital system is efficient but different. Learn the step-by-step process: registration挂号, lab work化验, prescriptions处方, and how to get insurance reimbursement. Public hospitals cost ¥50-200, while international clinics run ¥300-800 per visit.

Step 1 — Registration

Walk in, find the registration desk (挂号台), bring your passport. Most hospitals now have self-service kiosks with English options. Cost is typically ¥10-50 depending on the specialist tier.

Step 2 — See the Doctor

  • Bring any previous medical records and medication names
  • Use a translation app or bring a Chinese-speaking friend
  • Most major hospitals in tier-1 cities have English-speaking staff on the international floor
Tip: Public hospitals are 4-6× cheaper than international clinics and often have shorter wait times than you'd expect.

Once prescribed, take your prescription to the in-house pharmacy (药房). Most common medications are available over the counter. For prescriptions requiring controlled substances, the pharmacy may ask for your passport.
